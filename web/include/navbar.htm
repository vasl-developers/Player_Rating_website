Why a player rating system?

Many ASL players around the globe participate in tournaments. While tournament formats and player matchup processes vary considerably, many tournaments use a player ranking to determine who plays who.

The ASL Player Rating System, and it's forerunner, AREA, are expressly designed to help tournament managers perform this ranking.

The ASL Player Rating System is built on previous work and applications. It uses historical data from past tournaments assembled by Bruno Nitrosso and others, most recently at asl-area.org. It also uses the same methodology as the AREA site to derive the player ratings.

By clicking on the options shown on this page, a Tournament Director or anyone else can see an ordered list of ASL players.

Data from 2017 - 2020 tournaments is now being added to the data curated by Bruno Nitrosso up to 2017.
